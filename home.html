Development:

Chinese Zodiac Team Creator



Overview + Premise:

In CS1300, we were asked to create a list interface! I chose to create an app that is based off the legend of the Chinese Zodiac, where all the animals raced to impress the emperor and the first twelve animals earned a spot in the Zodiac. In my app, users can select which of the twelve Zodiac animals to add to their own Race Team, and view the combined number of celestial legs (and therefore race power) their team has. The final app is viewable here.




Goal of App

The goal of the application is to enable users to create a race team from the twelve Chinese Zodiac Animals. They can filter the animals by Celestial Element (Metal, Water, Fire, Earth, Wood) and Yin/Yang Alignment, as well as sort the animals by either Race Order (the order in which the animals completed the legendary race) or Reverse Race Order ( so users can see which animals were fastest or slowest). Users can select or unselect animals for their team, and view the combined number of legs the team would have, a.k.a. the animal power of the chosen race team.

Value to Users

This app is useful to any user interested in learning more about the Chinese Zodiac animals. While many people are well-versed in Western Zodiacs and horoscopes, much less people are well educated about the Chinese Zodiac. Of the population of people who are aware of the Chinese Zodiac, most of them only know of the existence of the animals, but are not familiar with the particular subcategories of characteristics that are integral to the formation of the Zodiac. Such important characteristics include the animal's Celestial Element and Yin/Yang alignment, both of which I created filters for in my app so users can explore the specific animals in each category.



Usability Principles Considered

Visual Style Cohesiveness

  • With how prominent and recognizable traditional Chinese color schemes are (reds, golds, etc.) it was important for me to have a strong visual style guide. I made sure my entire app adhered to a memorable color palette that was reminiscent of traditional Chinese ornamentation and aesthetics.

Accessibility + Learnability

  • I wanted to make the app as easy to use as possible, and made sure the contrast between the text and background colors was enough to make everything readable.
  • The dropdown menus and filters, as well as the buttons and cards, are all consistent in visual design and function throughot the app to ensure ease of use and learnability.
  • I used my jade green from my color palette as the accent color, knowing that it would differentiate itself drastically from the other warmer colors and stand out as a call-to-action color. Therefore, the buttons to reset all filters and add/remove animals from the user's selected race team were green.
